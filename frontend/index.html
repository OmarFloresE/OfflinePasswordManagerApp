What Constitutes a Strong Password?

  • Length: A minimum of 12 to 16 characters is often recommended.
  • Complexity: Should include a mix of:
    • Uppercase letters (A-Z)
    • Lowercase letters (a-z)
    • Numbers (0-9)
    • Special characters (e.g., !, @, #, $, %, ^, &, *)
  • Unpredictability: Avoid predictable patterns and sequences, such as "12345", "qwerty", or "abcdef".
  • No Personal Information: Do not use easily available information like your name, birth date, or common words that can be found in dictionaries.
  • Uniqueness: Each account should have its own unique password. Avoid reusing passwords across multiple accounts.
  • Memorability: While complex and unpredictable, it should be memorable. A passphrase can be a good approach, e.g., "BlueCoffeeMug@Sunset2024!".
  • Avoid Common Substitutions: Passwords that replace letters with similar-looking numbers or symbols are often not as secure as you might think.
  • Avoid Sequential Keyboard Paths: Passwords like "1qaz2wsx" or "1q2w3e4r" can be easily guessed.
